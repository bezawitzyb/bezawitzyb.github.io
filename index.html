/ 01
Le Wagon Final Project · March 2026

NoShowShield

End-to-end ML system predicting hotel booking cancellations on 119,390 real bookings. XGBoost classifier (AUC ≥ 0.86) feeds a Poisson-Binomial overbooking optimiser; SHAP explainability surfaces per-booking risk drivers and global feature importance. FastAPI + Streamlit, containerised on Google Cloud Run with sub-2 s response.

Packhunt · Product & Engineering · 2021–2025

Walraven Channel Selector

B2B web product that runs live structural calculations for fixing-rail selection. Engineers input rail type, supports, and point / distributed loads; the tool returns maximum stress, deflection, and an auto-generated PDF report. Deployed across 20+ Walraven markets. Cut project completion from 2–5 days to 1–2 hours. Used usage analytics to retire five underperforming templates and concentrate effort on the two actually driving value.

Hi — I’m Bezawit.

I trained as an architect. That meant constantly thinking about how people interact with systems, spaces, and constraints. Eventually that way of thinking pulled me into product management, where I’ve spent the last five years building digital tools in construction and sustainability.

The question above has been shaping how I think about products lately. It’s what pushed me deeper into data science, machine learning, and generative AI. In March 2026 I completed Le Wagon’s 400-hour Data Science & AI bootcamp — learning how RAG, deep learning, and agentic systems actually work under the hood, not just at the API level.

What I bring is the ability to bridge worlds. Product strategy, stakeholder alignment, and systems thinking on one side; a working technical foundation in AI and data on the other.

I want to build products that sit at the intersection of human creativity and intelligent systems.
